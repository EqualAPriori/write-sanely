# Rewrite Playbooks

Use this module when local edits are not enough. Rebuild the paragraph around the claim, object, mechanism, evidence, boundary, and consequence.

## Technical Paragraph Rebuild

Sequence:

1. Claim: what is true?
2. Object: what model, metric, dataset, parameter, method, or system carries the claim?
3. Mechanism: how does it work?
4. Evidence: what number, source, or example supports it?
5. Boundary: where does it stop applying?
6. Consequence: what changes for the reader's understanding?

Before:

> This section reveals a nuanced shift in the public health landscape: wastewater dashboards are no longer merely tools, but increasingly central actors in an ever-evolving ecosystem of preparedness.

After:

> Wastewater surveillance can detect a rising viral load before clinics report more positive tests. It still depends on sampling coverage, lab turnaround, and a reporting threshold that local officials can act on.

## Meta-Commentary To Claim

Before:

> This paragraph establishes the contrast before the next section names it.

After:

> Real-time access has broadened faster than access to validated outcome data.

Rule: delete the description of the rhetorical move and make the move.

## Defensive Scope Stack To Positive Scope

Before:

> The set is illustrative, not exhaustive; vendor claims are attributed, not endorsed; Company X is one node, not the center.

After:

> Placement reflects public products, papers, and documented deployments.

Rule: one positive evidence rule beats three defensive disclaimers.

## Parenthetical Dump To Evidence Flow

Before:

> The tax estimate did not survive the robustness checks (of 1,842 firms, 312 switched reporting categories after the rule change, and the headline effect disappeared once the model included firm fixed effects).

After:

> The tax estimate did not survive the robustness checks. Of 1,842 firms, 312 switched reporting categories after the rule change. Once the model included firm fixed effects, the headline effect was no longer distinguishable from zero.

Rule: if the number matters, put it in a sentence.

## Clever Phrase To Mechanism

Before:

> The far tail listens to one number.

After:

> In the long-run simulation, the top-tail concentration measure changes mainly with the top bracket rate.

Rule: if a clever phrase is the memorable part, check whether it displaced the claim.

## Decorative Metaphor To Literal Claim

Before:

> Wealth of that kind is invisible to a profit tax at any rate, the way a silent engine is invisible to a microphone.

After:

> A flow tax cannot reach gains retained inside a holding structure until those gains are distributed or realized at the taxable level.

Rule: keep metaphor only when it helps the reader understand the literal claim faster.

## Generic Conclusion To Decision

Before:

> These findings show the complexity of the debate and point toward a more nuanced path forward.

After:

> The practical decision is whether the policy goal is aggregate compression, top-tail compression, or revenue collection from assets that produce no taxable flow.

Rule: a conclusion should add a boundary, consequence, decision, or open question.

## AI Summary To Authored Synthesis

Before:

> Overall, this highlights the importance of collaboration across the ecosystem to unlock the full potential of clean energy.

After:

> The grid problem is narrower than the slogan suggests. Developers need interconnection studies; utilities need load forecasts they can defend to regulators; public agencies can help most where shared queue data reduces duplicated planning work.

Rule: replace abstract consensus with actors, incentives, and constraints.
